Understanding the Formulas to calculate body fat

US Navy Method

For Men: 495 / (1.0324 - 0.19077 * log10(waist - neck) + 0.15456 * log10(height)) - 450

For Women: 495 / (1.29579 - 0.35004 * log10(waist + hip - neck) + 0.22100 * log10(height)) - 450

The US Navy method estimates body fat percentage using measurements of the neck, waist, and height for men, and the neck, waist, hip, and height for women. It is considered accurate and practical as it only requires a few measurements.

BMI Method

For Men: 1.20 * bmi + 0.23 * age - 16.2

For Women: 1.20 * bmi + 0.23 * age - 5.4

The BMI method estimates body fat percentage using the Body Mass Index (BMI) and the age of the individual. It is a simple and quick method, but it may not be as accurate as other methods as it does not take into account the distribution of fat and muscle in the body.

Body Fat Mass

Body Fat Mass = weight * bodyFatNavy / 100

This formula calculates the mass of body fat by multiplying the total body weight by the body fat percentage calculated using the US Navy method.

Lean Body Mass

Lean Body Mass = weight - bodyFatMass

This formula calculates the mass of lean body tissue by subtracting the body fat mass from the total body weight.

Body Fat Required to Lose to Reach Ideal Weight

Body Fat Required to Lose = bodyFatMass - weight * idealBodyFat / 100

This formula calculates the amount of body fat required to lose to reach the ideal body weight. It subtracts the ideal body fat mass (calculated by multiplying the total body weight by the ideal body fat percentage) from the current body fat mass.
