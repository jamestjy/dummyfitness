Understanding the formulas for calculating Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

For Men: BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A + 5

For Women: BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A - 161

The Mifflin-St Jeor Equation is considered the most accurate for calculating BMR. It estimates the energy needed to maintain body functions while at rest. Separate equations exist for men and women, accounting for weight, height, and age.

Revised Harris-Benedict Equation

For Men: BMR = 13.397W + 4.799H - 5.677A + 88.362

For Women: BMR = 9.247W + 3.098H - 4.330A + 447.593

The Harris-Benedict Equation, revised in 1984, provides an estimate of BMR. It considers weight, height, and age. Like the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation, separate equations exist for men and women.

Katch-McArdle Formula

BMR = 370 + 21.6(1 - F)W

The Katch-McArdle Formula incorporates lean body mass by considering body fat percentage. It provides a more personalized BMR estimate. Unlike the previous equations, it does not directly account for weight, height, or age.

Units and Legends

Units: Kilograms (kg) for weight, centimeters (cm) for height, and years for age.

Where: (W) is body weight, (H) is body height, (A) is age, and (F) represents body fat percentage.
